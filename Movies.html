In Black Panther (2018), T’Challa returns home after his father’s death to take his place as king, but his reign is challenged by Erik Killmonger, a rival with a painful past and a radical vision for Wakanda’s future. T’Challa must navigate tradition, responsibility, and justice to protect his people and define what kind of leader he will be.

In Black Panther: Wakanda Forever (2022), the nation mourns the loss of T’Challa and faces rising global pressure for access to vibranium. As Queen Ramonda, Shuri, and the Wakandan people fight to defend their sovereignty, a new threat surfaces from the underwater kingdom of Talokan, led by the powerful Namor. In the midst of grief and uncertainty, a new Black Panther must rise to carry on the legacy and protect Wakanda’s future.
